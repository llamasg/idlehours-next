## What Idle Hours Is

Idle Hours is a game discovery engine with an editorial voice.

Every page on the site — a review, a list, a puzzle game, a sticky 
note, a mood guide — exists to help someone find their next game. 
That is the only test that matters. Not whether it's well-written. 
Not whether it's beautifully designed. Whether it ends with a game 
being surfaced.

The editorial layer (Beth's writing, the illustration standard) is 
what earns the trust that makes the discovery meaningful. The games 
(Game Sense, Street Date, Shelf Price, and the arcade) are what turns 
a visit into a daily habit. The affiliate links are the infrastructure 
that funds it — not an add-on, not an afterthought.

These three things are not in tension. They are the same thing 
expressed differently.

---

## The North Star

**Those extra hours you didn't plan to spend — and didn't want to end.**

The idle hours feeling is not about genre. It is not about difficulty. 
It is not about being cosy. It is about a quality of absorption — the 
feeling that time is well spent even when nothing is being achieved.

Balatro is an Idle Hours game. Celeste is an Idle Hours game. Cairn 
is an Idle Hours game. A slow horror game where the tension is 
atmospheric and the world is handcrafted is an Idle Hours game. 
Fortnite is not.

The question is never "is this cosy?" The question is:

> Does this game create idle hours?

---

## The Idle Hours Test

Ask these before covering or recommending any game:

| Question | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Can you set it down without feeling punished? | Auto-saves, no decay mechanics, generous checkpoints |
| Does it reward presence over performance? | Valuable even if you're not progressing efficiently |
| Is it made with evident craft and love? | Intentional mechanics, considered art, not corporate |
| Does it respect the player's time and intelligence? | No dark patterns, no FOMO, no pay-to-win |
| Would someone describe it as hard to put down? | Compulsion from genuine engagement, not manufactured addiction |

## What We Cover

We are not a cosy games site. We are a discovery engine for games 
that have the idle hours feeling. That includes:

- Classic absorbing worlds (Stardew, Animal Crossing, Minecraft)
- Meditative challenge (Cairn, Jusant, Subnautica)
- One-more-run magic (Balatro, Slay the Spire, Vampire Survivors)
- Unlikely comfort (Celeste, Hades, Into the Breach)
- Quiet narratives (Disco Elysium, Unpacking, A Short Hike)
- Atmospheric horror (games where dread is the texture, not the punishment)
- Craft and building (Dwarf Fortress, Factorio, RimWorld)
- Idle and incremental (genuine idle games, not skinner boxes)

We do not cover:
- Live service extractors
- Pure anxiety machines (ranked competitive, social punishment loops)
- Games cynically labelled cosy where the label is marketing not design
- Empty aesthetic — pretty visuals with hollow mechanics

## Who We're Writing For

Three readers find Idle Hours. They share one thing: they want to 
feel comfortable with how they're spending their time.

**The Burned-Out Former Gamer**
Used to love games. It stopped being fun — obligations, FOMO, 
grinding. Needs permission to remember why games were good. We 
tell them: these games are worth your time, and your time is worth 
protecting.

**The Enthusiast at the Edge of Their Genre**
Knows Stardew. Wants to know about Cairn. Wants their taste 
validated and expanded. Comes for discovery and for articulation — 
to have their feelings about a game expressed well.

**The Complete Newcomer**
Doesn't identify as a gamer. Needs a guide who won't condescend. 
These become the most loyal readers if we earn them.

## The Discovery Engine Principle

This is the organising principle behind every product decision.

Every page ends with a game being surfaced. This is not a content 
strategy — it is the architecture of the site. A review surfaces the 
game being reviewed and related recommendations. A mood guide 
surfaces games for that mood. A puzzle game on the arcade ends 
with a "you might like..." card. A sticky note links to a game page. 
A list post links to affiliate storefronts.

**Affiliate links are infrastructure, not monetisation bolted on.**

They are the last step of a discovery journey that starts with trust 
and ends with a purchase. A reader who trusts a recommendation 
follows a link. A reader who feels sold to never comes back.

## Affiliate links — how the money actually works

The /games library hub is the commercial engine of the site.
Every game listed there has affiliate links to storefronts 
(GOG, Humble Bundle, Green Man Gaming, Fanatical). When 
someone clicks through and buys, we earn commission.

The funnel is always the same regardless of where it starts:

  Content or puzzle mentions a game
        ↓
  Game title links to the lightbox
        ↓
  Lightbox shows cover art, vibe, platforms, editorial take
        ↓
  Reader clicks the affiliate buy link
        ↓
  Commission earned

This means every page on the site — a blog post, a puzzle 
result, a mood guide, a sticky note — is a top-of-funnel 
entry point into this conversion flow. No page is decorative.
If it mentions a game and doesn't link to the lightbox, 
it's leaving money on the table.

The library hub (~400 curated games) is intentionally small 
and high quality. We do not list every game we cover in 
puzzles — only games that pass the Idle Hours test earn a 
lightbox entry. This protects the editorial trust that makes 
the affiliate click feel like a recommendation, not an ad.

Status: affiliate programme applications (GOG, Humble, GMG, 
Fanatical) are the current blocker. Infrastructure is ready.
Once partner codes are confirmed, a single script appends 
affiliate parameters to all existing store links in the 
database simultaneously.

The single metric that predicts all revenue outcomes:
**Total daily active players across the arcade.**

More players → more trust → more affiliate conversions → more 
sponsored review interest → more revenue. Every feature decision 
should be evaluated against whether it moves this number.

## Financial Goals & Monetisation

Target: £2,000–£3,000/month. Slow, careful build.

**Revenue streams in priority order:**

1. **Affiliate links** — Humble Bundle, GOG, Green Man Gaming, 
   Fanatical. Every game recommendation links out. Never recommend 
   something for commission rate alone. If a better non-affiliated 
   option exists, mention it.

2. **Sponsored reviews** — Editorial-led, clearly disclosed. We cover 
   the game on our terms or we don't cover it. Rate card tied to 
   traffic milestones.

3. **Deals & sales page** — High commercial intent. Planned 
   post-core-build. Directly serves the audience's appetite for value.

4. **Future: premium accounts / cosmetics** — Only when DAU justifies 
   the infrastructure complexity. Not before.

**Disclosure standard:** Affiliate relationships disclosed clearly, 
not buried. Sponsored content labelled as such at the top of the 
post, not the bottom.

## Tone of Voice

A friend who plays a lot of games and genuinely wants to help you 
find your next favourite. Warm, considered, unhurried. Never 
condescending. Never over-hyped. Notices what makes things good 
and says it clearly.

Warm but not cutesy. Confident but not po-faced.

**Never write:**
- "deep dive", "masterpiece", "hidden gem" (show it instead)
- "game-changing", "a must-play", "iconic", "journey"
- "cozy" — this is a UK site, use "cosy"
- Scored verdicts that feel like a number out of nowhere

**Write instead:**
- "Stardew Valley is the game most people mean when they say 
  they want something cosy — and it earns that"
- "Balatro will steal your Friday night and make you grateful 
  for the theft"
- "8/10 — the kind of game you'll still be thinking about on 
  your commute"

## Content — The Four Tests

Every post must do at least one of these:

1. Help someone find a game that's right for them right now — 
   not just "this is good" but "this is good for you, in this 
   mood, today"
2. Make someone feel seen — validate that playing at your own 
   pace isn't a lesser form of gaming
3. Teach something genuinely useful that stays useful after 
   the tab is closed
4. Create a moment of calm — the writing itself should feel 
   like the games we cover

If a post doesn't do any of these, do not publish it.

## The Games

All three games are daily puzzle format with share card mechanics 
for organic distribution.

- **Game Sense** — daily quiz, 1000pt max, five RPG ranks: 
  Skill Issue → First Playthrough → Knows the Lore → 
  Final Save → Legendary
- **Street Date** — drag-and-drop chronological ordering, 
  7 cards, 5 guesses, two-tier hint system
- **Shelf Price** — streak-based pricing game

Badge/reward illustration direction: board game prop style 
(Wingspan/Everdell/Root aesthetic). Thick outlines, flat colour, 
bold shapes. Circular badges, white outlines, 
artwork can spill outside the circle.
